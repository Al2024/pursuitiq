REQUEST FOR PROPOSAL - HOSPITAL RENOVATION PROJECT

Project Title: Metropolitan General Hospital - Emergency Department Expansion
Client: Metropolitan Healthcare System
Location: 123 Medical Center Drive, Downtown Metro City
Project Budget: $8.5 - $12.5 million
Project Duration: 18 months

PROJECT OVERVIEW:
Metropolitan Healthcare System is seeking qualified architectural and engineering firms to provide comprehensive design and construction administration services for the expansion and renovation of our Emergency Department. The project involves a 25,000 sq ft addition to our existing facility, including new patient rooms, waiting areas, and support spaces.

SCOPE OF SERVICES:
1. Architectural design and documentation
2. Structural engineering for new construction and existing building modifications
3. MEP engineering (HVAC, electrical, plumbing, medical gas systems)
4. Civil engineering for site improvements
5. Interior design and FF&E coordination
6. Construction administration and inspection

REQUIRED DISCIPLINES:
- Lead Architecture
- Structural Engineering
- Mechanical Engineering (HVAC)
- Electrical Engineering
- Plumbing Engineering
- Civil Engineering
- Landscape Architecture
- Interior Design
- Medical Planning (consultant)
- Acoustical Engineering
- Lighting Design

TECHNICAL REQUIREMENTS:
- LEED Gold certification required
- HIPAA compliance for all patient areas
- Seismic design per IBC 2021
- Fire protection per NFPA codes
- Accessibility per ADA 2010 with 2013 updates
- Infection control design standards
- Emergency power systems for critical areas
- Medical gas and vacuum systems
- Nurse call and communication systems

POTENTIAL RISKS:
- Working in occupied healthcare facility
- Strict regulatory compliance requirements
- Complex coordination with hospital operations
- Limited construction hours (7 AM - 6 PM weekdays only)
- Phased construction to maintain operations
- Specialized medical equipment integration
- Infection control during construction
- Utility shutdown coordination
- Historic building preservation requirements
- Community noise and disruption concerns

EVALUATION CRITERIA:

- Fee proposal (15%)
- Interview performance (10%)
